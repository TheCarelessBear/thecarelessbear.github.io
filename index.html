Questions for the chef
We've heard
these before.
I cook by feel and everything works out. Why do I need this?+
Consistent food and a profitable business are two very different things. A perfect dish can still lose you money if ingredient prices went up and your menu price didn't move. Your cooking keeps the food consistent — mar.gins keeps the money healthy underneath it.
I don't know my exact measurements. Do I need them?+
No. Enter your best guess. Even rough numbers give you directionally correct costs that are infinitely more useful than nothing. "About a handful of cheese" is a start. You can refine over time.
What's a good food cost percentage?+
The industry standard for most food businesses is 28–35%. Below 28% means you might be able to raise prices. Above 38% means that dish is quietly bleeding you — raise the price or reduce the portion.
Is my data private?+
Everything you enter stays on your device. There's no account, no server, nothing sent anywhere. We literally cannot see your data.
Another app I have to use? Really?+
You set it up once, enter your recipes once, and it tells you right then if you're making money. After that you only open it when costs change or you add something new. It's less of an app and more like a calculator that remembers everything.
Who's in the kitchen?+
mar.gins was built by a local entrepreneur from Markham, Ontario — after too many conversations with food business owners who had no idea if they were actually making money. It's early stage, free, and your feedback shapes every new feature.
